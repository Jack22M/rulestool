Summary Notes

Chapter 9

  • A player must not hold their stick dangerously
  • Play must not handle or intefere with other players stick or clothing
  • Players must not play the ball with the back of their stick
  • Players may play the ball in a controlled manner in any part of the field including above their shoulder as long as it is not dangerous
  • Players must not play the ball dangerously. Dangerous play is also counted if the opposing player has to take legitimite evasive action
  • Players must not intneitonally raise the ball from a hit unless it is a shot a goal
  • Players must not kick, use their feet or any other part of the body. It can also be an offence if it touches the body if it is not dangerous and there is a disadvantage to the other team.
  • Rule 9.12: Players must not obstruct an opponent who is attempting to play the ball.

Players obstruct if they:

- back into an opponent [and/or] physically interfere with the stick or body of an opponent

- sheild the ball from a legitimate tackle with their stick or any part of their body

Note: just because there is a noise it does not mean there is always an infringement

  • Players must not tackkle unless there a in a decent position to play the ball without body contact
  • Players must not throw any object/equipment onto the field or at another player or umpire
  • Players must not delay play to gain benefit by time wasting
